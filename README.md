# Alice — Chief of Staff for AI Agents

> Your AI agent is smart but it doesn't know your world.
> [GBrain](https://github.com/garrytan/gbrain) fixes knowledge. Alice fixes judgment.

Alice is the intelligence and personality layer for founder-facing AI agents. Where infrastructure tools give your agent memory and retrieval, Alice gives it **calibrated judgment** — the ability to prioritize like you, escalate like you, and draft like you, across every system you actually use.

The IP is the calibration process itself: a structured onboarding that turns a generic LLM into an operator that understands your world model, your communication style, your trust boundaries, and your definition of "dropped ball." In 45 minutes, you go from a blank agent to a chief of staff that produces real operational output.

## Quick Start

### Prerequisites

- A Slack workspace you admin (for app install)
- Gmail account (for email triage)
- Optional: Notion workspace, Obsidian vault, Telegram group

### Step 1: Clone and set up

```bash
git clone https://github.com/bouquet-garden/alice-chief-of-staff-starter.git
cd alice-chief-of-staff-starter
bash scripts/alice-setup.sh
```

The setup wizard detects your environment and scaffolds the workspace:

### Step 2: Bootstrap your agent

Start your preferred agent runtime and paste the bootstrap prompt. Alice will begin the onboarding conversation:

```
> Alice: "I'm Alice, your chief of staff. I'm going to spend about 45 minutes
  learning how you work so I can start reducing your operational load today.

  Before we start — I can read, reconcile, draft, and prepare updates across
  your systems. I will NOT send any external messages unless you explicitly
  allow that. For week 1, I recommend Draft + Internal Actions only.

  Sound good? Let's start with: what are the top 3 outcomes you need this week?"
```

### Step 3: First magic moment

By minute 40, Alice will produce your first real operational output — typically a stale-thread recovery brief or a follow-up draft packet. This is not a demo. These are real threads from your real inbox.

## Positioning: Alice vs GBrain

Alice and [GBrain](https://github.com/garrytan/gbrain) solve different problems. They are complementary, not competitive.

**GBrain** is knowledge infrastructure — it gives your agent durable memory, semantic search, and retrieval. It answers: *"What do I know?"*

**Alice** is the intelligence layer — it gives your agent calibrated judgment, operational workflows, and founder-specific behavior. It answers: *"What should I do about it?"*

| Dimension | GBrain | Alice |
|---|---|---|
| **Core function** | Store, index, retrieve knowledge | Prioritize, decide, draft, reconcile |
| **Primary output** | Retrieved context, search results | Operating briefs, drafts, alerts, reconciliation |
| **Personalization** | Knowledge graph structure | Behavioral calibration (style, autonomy, escalation) |
| **Learning model** | Knowledge accumulation | Judgment refinement (corrections → rules → playbooks) |
| **Trust model** | Data freshness + provenance | Autonomy ladder + approval gates |
| **Onboarding** | Connect knowledge sources | 45-min calibration conversation |
| **Handles conflict** | Stores conflicting info | Detects contradictions, proposes resolution |
| **Stars** | 7,400+ | New |

**How they work together:**

```
GBrain provides:     "The ACME thread was last touched 9 days ago.
                      Related Notion task is still marked In Progress.
                      Slack message from @jordan said 'done' on Apr 4."

Alice provides:      "This is a P1 contradiction. Ball is with us.
                      Notion is execution truth, so keep In Progress.
                      Draft follow-up prepared. Escalate in today's brief."
```

GBrain stores and retrieves. Alice decides, prioritizes, drafts, reconciles, and compounds.

## Philosophy: Intelligence Not Infrastructure

Most AI agent frameworks focus on giving agents better tools — more integrations, faster retrieval, larger context windows. These are important. But they solve the wrong bottleneck for operational work.

The bottleneck is not "can my agent access my email." The bottleneck is "does my agent know which email matters, who owns the response, whether the thread contradicts what Notion says, and how I want to be told about it." That is judgment, not infrastructure. Judgment requires knowing the founder's world model — their priorities, their trust boundaries, their communication style, their definition of urgent. No amount of RAG or tool-calling solves this without calibration.

Alice's thesis is that the calibration process itself is the product. The 45-minute onboarding is not a setup chore — it is the core feature. Every question Alice asks during onboarding directly improves the precision of every output it produces afterward. This is why Alice compounds: each correction becomes a rule, each rule becomes a playbook, and each playbook reduces the correction rate further. Infrastructure gives your agent knowledge. Alice gives your agent the judgment to use it.

*Full essay: [`docs/philosophy/intelligence-not-infrastructure.md`](docs/philosophy/intelligence-not-infrastructure.md)*

## Numbers: 30-Day Backtest Estimate

These numbers represent what Alice would have caught in a conservative 30-day backtest over a typical founder's operational data. They are not projections — they are based on the patterns Alice detects in historical email, Slack, Notion, and transcript data during onboarding.

| Metric | 30-Day Estimate | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| **Stale threads surfaced** | 12-18 | High (direct detection) |
| **High-risk commitments caught** | 4-7 | High (ownership + staleness) |
| **Cross-source contradictions flagged** | 5-10 | Moderate (depends on source coverage) |
| **Draft responses prepared** | 20-30 | High (mechanical) |
| **Meeting prep dossiers generated** | 8-12 | High (calendar-driven) |
| **Follow-ups recovered within 24h** | 60-70% of stale threads | Moderate |
| **Hours saved per week** | 3-5 hours | Moderate (varies by volume) |
| **Correction rate (week 1 → week 4)** | ~40% → ~15% | Moderate (calibration-dependent) |

### What these numbers mean in practice

- **12-18 stale threads** = 12-18 times you would have said "I forgot about that" or "I thought someone was handling this." Alice catches them before they become problems.
- **4-7 high-risk commitments** = external promises (to clients, partners, investors) that were aging past their SLA with no owner. These are the ones that damage relationships.
- **3-5 hours/week** = not from automation magic, but from not having to reconstruct context yourself. Alice already knows the thread history, the Notion state, and the last meeting discussion. You just approve or edit.

These are conservative. We deliberately undercount because trust requires honesty about what the system actually catches versus what it might catch in ideal conditions.

## What Alice Is — and Is Not

### Alice is
- A chief-of-staff operating layer for AI agents
- A system for judgment, context, memory, reconciliation, and workflow execution
- A productized onboarding and calibration model
- A durable operating model that compounds from real usage

### Alice is not
- A fully autonomous executive replacement
- A promise that every integration works on day one
- A guarantee that all workflows are automated before configuration
- A generic assistant with a fancy persona

### The right mental model

Alice is like hiring a sharp operator who learns your systems, sets an operating contract, creates durable context, reduces drift, and prepares work before taking irreversible action. Week 1 is about calibration and trust-building. Week 4 is where compounding becomes visible.

### Onboarding phases at a glance

| Phase | Time | What happens | Artifact produced |
|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery | 10-20 min | Alice learns your priorities, pain points, systems | `USER.md`, `GOALS.md` |
| Operating Contract | 5-10 min | Set autonomy, escalation, quiet hours, style | `alice-settings.yml` |
| Source Mapping | 10-20 min | Define which system is truth for what | `SOURCES_OF_TRUTH.md` |
| First Magic Moment | 15-30 min | One real operational output from your data | Operating brief or draft packet |
| Calibration Loop | Ongoing | Corrections and adjustments improve precision | Updated settings + new rules |

## ROI Framework

Alice measures value in four buckets. Every feature maps to at least one.

| Bucket | What to track | Week 1 target |
|---|---|---|
| **Dropped balls reduced** | Stale commitments caught, missing owners surfaced | At least 1 real recovery |
| **Time saved** | Brief prep, draft prep, meeting prep, triage time | 2-3 hours |
| **Trust and clarity** | Correction rate, false positive rate, accepted recommendations | Trust increasing, not decreasing |
| **Compounding** | New rules extracted, playbooks created, settings improvements | At least 1 new rule from usage |

```
Example: Week 1 ROI Snapshot

- stale commitments detected: 6
- missing owners surfaced: 3
- contradiction alerts surfaced: 4

Founder time saved:
- meeting prep saved: ~45 min
- draft prep saved: ~30 min
- follow-up triage saved: ~35 min

Quality:
- intervention precision: 4/6 high-value
- correction rate: 2/6
- false positives: 1/6

Compounding:
- new rules captured: 2
- settings changes made: 3
- repeatable workflow promoted to playbook: 1
```

## Contributing

### Mental model for contributors

Treat the system as three layers (Behavior, Knowledge, Execution). A good contribution improves at least one layer without breaking the others.

### Delivery standard

Every new module should include:
- Product intent (why this exists)
- Input/output contracts (what goes in, what comes out)
- Settings and defaults (what the user controls)
- Safety gates (what requires approval)
- Confidence/provenance behavior (how sure is Alice, and why)
- Concrete examples (not just schemas — real output)
- Implemented-vs-planned labeling with evidence links

### Reference repos

- [GBrain](https://github.com/garrytan/gbrain) — knowledge infrastructure, durable personal knowledge
- [gstack](https://github.com/garrytan/gstack) — practical execution/tooling structure
- [Hermes Agent](https://github.com/nousresearch/hermes-agent) — productized agent documentation model

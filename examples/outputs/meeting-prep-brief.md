## Talking points

- **Lead with the data room.** Jason asked for it. Showing you've already
  delivered it before the call demonstrates responsiveness — important
  given the Northfield situation (do not let a pattern of slow follow-up
  form with investors).

- **Enterprise NRR is the headline number.** 118% NRR in enterprise is
  strong for a pre-Series A company. Lead with this when discussing
  retention.

- **Canopy framing.** If Jason brings up Canopy, use the "communication
  layer vs. workflow layer" framing. Aria reads and interprets; Canopy
  executes. They're complementary. This is better than a defensive "we
  don't compete" answer.

- **Be specific on timeline.** Investors respond to founders who know
  their process. "We're targeting a close by end of May, currently in
  diligence with two firms, and plan to make a lead decision by May 15"
  is better than "we're in the process."

## Things to avoid

- **Do not volunteer the Northfield delay.** If it comes up in diligence
  (e.g., customer references), address it honestly but do not proactively
  raise a 23-day dropped ball with an investor.

- **Do not oversell SMB.** The 7% SMB number is small and the retention
  is weaker. If Lisa asks about it, frame it as "deliberate pilots to
  test the wedge" rather than a growth segment.

- **Do not give a valuation number unless asked.** Let them anchor first.
  If pressed, say "we're calibrating based on diligence conversations
  this month."
